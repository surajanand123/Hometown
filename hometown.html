Top 10 Tourist Places in Patna

Patna

Patna, capital of Bihar, is a city steeped in history and culture. Situated on the banks of the Ganges River, it boasts renowned tourist attractions, ancient temples, universities, and commercial hubs. It's a vibrant metropolis with a rich heritage, attracting visitors and scholars from around the world.

Mahatma Gandhi Setu

The Mahatma Gandhi Setu is one of India's longest river bridges, spanning the Ganges River near Patna, Bihar. Named after Mahatma Gandhi, it connects Patna to Hajipur and serves as a vital transportation link. Completed in 1982, it facilitates the movement of vehicles and people across the river, fostering regional connectivity.

Takht Sri Patna Sahib

Takht Sri Patna Sahib, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is a revered Sikh shrine in Patna, Bihar. It marks the birthplace of Guru Gobind Singh, the tenth Sikh Guru. The gurdwara, adorned with white marble and golden domes, exudes serenity and spirituality. Inside, the sanctum houses the Guru Granth Sahib, the holy scripture of Sikhism. Pilgrims from around the world visit to pay homage and seek blessings. The complex also includes museums, a langar hall serving free meals, and accommodation facilities. It stands as a symbol of Sikh faith and heritage, embodying the teachings of compassion, equality, and service to humanity.

Patna Museum

The Patna Museum, established in 1917, is a treasure trove of ancient artifacts and historical relics located in Patna, Bihar. Its vast collection showcases the rich cultural heritage of the region, including sculptures, coins, paintings, and textiles dating back to ancient times. Highlights include the Didarganj Yakshi, an exquisite stone sculpture from the Maurya period, and the iconic fossilized tree trunk from the Tertiary period. The museum also houses galleries dedicated to Bihar's natural history, archaeology, and art. With its impressive exhibits and informative displays, the museum offers visitors a fascinating journey through the history and heritage of Bihar.

Kumhrar

Kumhrar, located in Patna, Bihar, is an archaeological site known for its ancient ruins dating back to the Maurya and Gupta periods. Excavations have revealed remnants of the ancient city of Pataliputra, including a pillared hall believed to be part of the Mauryan palace. The site also features remnants of ancient monasteries, stupas, and residential areas, providing valuable insights into the urban planning and lifestyle of ancient India. Kumhrar is a significant historical and archaeological destination, attracting scholars, historians, and tourists keen on exploring the rich heritage of Pataliputra, once a thriving political and cultural center of ancient India.

Golghar

Golghar, a prominent landmark in Patna, Bihar, is an iconic granary built by the British in 1786. Its unique beehive-shaped structure stands 29 meters tall and has a circumference of over 100 meters. Initially constructed to store grain during times of famine, it now offers panoramic views of the city and the Ganges River from its top. Despite its utilitarian origin, Golghar has become a symbol of Patna's architectural heritage and resilience. Visitors flock to admire its distinctive design and learn about its historical significance, making it a must-visit destination for both locals and tourists alike.

Agam Kuan

Agam Kuan, translating to "Unfathomable Well," is an ancient historical site located in Patna, Bihar. This cylindrical-shaped well, believed to date back to the Maurya period, is surrounded by myth and legend. Its depth is still unknown, as attempts to measure it have been unsuccessful due to the water's turbidity. Legend has it that Emperor Ashoka used it as a place of torture and execution. Despite its grim past, Agam Kuan is now a revered site, attracting visitors intrigued by its mysterious history and architectural significance, offering a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of ancient India.

Patna Planetarium

The Patna Planetarium, established in 1993, is a state-of-the-art facility located in Patna, Bihar, dedicated to astronomy education and public outreach. It features a domed theater equipped with advanced projection technology, offering captivating celestial shows on topics ranging from the solar system to distant galaxies. The planetarium also hosts interactive exhibits, workshops, and lectures to engage visitors of all ages in the wonders of the universe. With its immersive presentations and informative programs, the Patna Planetarium serves as a beacon of scientific education and inspiration, fostering a deeper appreciation for the cosmos among its visitors.

Padri Ki Haveli

Padri ki Haveli, also known as St. Mary's Church, is a historic landmark in Patna, Bihar. Built in the early 18th century , it is one of the oldest churches in the region. The architecture reflects a blend of European and Indian styles, with ornate arches, intricate woodwork, and stained glass windows. The church holds significant historical importance, having witnessed key events during the British colonial period. Today, it continues to serve as a place of worship for the local Christian community and attracts visitors interested in its rich architectural heritage and cultural significance in Patna's history.

Sanjay Gandhi Biological Park

The Sanjay Gandhi Biological Park, commonly known as Patna Zoo, is a renowned zoological park located in Patna, Bihar. Established in 1969, it spans over 153 acres and is home to a diverse array of flora and fauna. The park features spacious enclosures housing various species of mammals, birds, and reptiles, including the rare one-horned rhinoceros and Royal Bengal tiger. In addition to its conservation efforts, the zoo offers educational programs, nature trails, and recreational facilities for visitors. With its emphasis on biodiversity conservation and environmental education, the Sanjay Gandhi Biological Park is a popular destination for both locals and tourists.
